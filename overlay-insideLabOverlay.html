Inside the Lab

A glimpse into our research environment, equipment, and daily operations.

Quantum Sensing Lab

Our quantum sensing facility hosts NV center diamond setups, Rydberg atom chambers, and optically pumped magnetometer systems. Precision optical tables with vibration isolation provide the stability needed for detecting ultra-weak magnetic and electric fields.

Atomic Magnetometer Station

Dedicated to brain signal measurement research, this station houses alkali vapor cells, laser systems for optical pumping, and magnetically shielded environments. We develop OPM sensors for non-invasive magnetoencephalography applications.

Quantum Computing Access

We have access to both D-Wave quantum annealing systems and the IQM Spark gate-based quantum computer. Our researchers develop and test quantum algorithms remotely, bridging theoretical design with real hardware execution.

Precision Optics Workshop

Our optics workshop features 5-axis mirror alignment systems and custom automation setups. This facility trains students in precision instrumentation skills applicable to both quantum research and industrial optical systems.

Electronics & Control

Custom electronics development for quantum experiments, including photodetector circuits, RF signal processing, laser controllers, and data acquisition systems. We design the specialized hardware that enables our sensing and measurement research.

Daily Life

Research at QMET spans from algorithm development on quantum computers to hands-on sensor construction and characterization. Weekly group meetings foster collaboration across our diverse research areas, preparing students for careers in both academia and industry.
